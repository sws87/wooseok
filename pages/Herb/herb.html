WOOSEOK SHUR
  works









Herb Lubalin    









Three 16” x 16” panels are designed to communicate a type designer, Herb Lubalin, works and contributions to typographic history. To work those panels as a set and as individual pieces, I used a wall mounted black boxes as a modular to give diversity and consistency. The order of arrangement can be changed freely or even they can be separated. The form of the modular comes from his signature rectangular frame style. But originally, the modular was the black flat boxes on white backgrounds, the contrast was extreme. To give more depth to the panels, slight shadows and subtle gradient on the background are added to make them be shown as 3 dimensional , even though squares are still flat.
